Trust - the problem blockchains solve‚Äã

Trust allows for coordination to scale. Without trust, the scope of things that people can do is greatly limited.

Think of building a home. Building a home by yourself would be laborious and hard. If your immediate family helped, the work could be done faster. The trust between your family allows everyone to agree to sacrfice today for tomorrow. In the future, should your brother or sister need a favor, you would be counted on to help.

Money allows for this trust to scale beyond kin networks. With money, you can save these IOUs and use them where you see fit. Since favors can be stored in money as well as relationships, this allows for people to specialize, allowing for more complex and higher quality goods and services. You won't need to know how to make bread, because you can buy it.

Trust, like energy is necessary to get things done. The Industrial Revolution helped unlock on-demand mechanical power in excess of what humans can provide. This allowed machines to produce more at scales never before seen.

As the scale of our ambitions grew, so did the need for collaboration. Financial systems evolved along side industrial development to provide a framework allowed for the coordination of trust via money.

Our industrial developement went as far as to provide mechanical work on demand in every home via electricty. Plug in an appliance, and elecicity will help power it.

However, modern finance isn't so flexible. Although we have ways to engage with finanaical systems, they rely on intermediaries.

The revolution of programmable blockchains is the following: Trust can be scaled and delivered on-demand like elecricty.

Why is scaling necessary?‚Äã

Scaling is necesary to lower the transactions costs on a blockchain. The lower the transaction costs, the more people can interact with each other in new ways. Lower transaction costs allows for new types of methods of interaction.

A decentralized twitter is not possible when it costs 5 USD per transaction. However, at 0.005 USD, this is more fesible.

How can we scale Ethereum?‚Äã

We can scale Ethereum through a variety of ways, the most popular being rollups.

Rollups are side networks that can perform computations, allow these computations to be verified, and settle on a main chain.

They are the most popular type of scaling solution because they the most compatiable with the etheruem virtual machine. This allows DApps perform the same functions on a rollup as they would on the mainnet with little alteratio at a fraction of the cost.

Additionally, rollups match nicely with how Ethereum plans to scale via sharding.

What is Sharding‚Äã

Sharding is the use of different storage on a network. Think of a plane of glass that is cracked. Each shard or piece represents storage.

For Ethereum, there are 64 shards. One of these is the execution network where computation occurs.

The Merge, referred to the event where the running execution chain merged on the sharded chain. Think of it like how Voltron robots combine to form one machine, while all still working.

Economics vs Math‚Äã

Problem: How do verify computations to guard against cheats?

Method 1: Economics incentives, with what essentially looks like posting a bond and a challenge period to incentivize investigation

Method 2: Math, with the ability to prove something is true without revealing more than the necessary information.

The first one is cheaper, and useful when the mathematical techniques are not known or the method to process is too expensive.

The second is superior long term as long as the costs are low and the method is rigorous enough to not be wrong within the stated objective.

The tradeoffs between these two methods is examined in the study of cryptoeconomics and security work. Cryptoeconomics is the study of using economic incentives to provide guarentees about an application. This application can be a network like Bitcoin or Ethereum, a decentralized application, a rollup or more.

Cryptoeconomics form a bedrock for mechanism design, which can be thought of as reverse game theory: how does the game creator design a game where the player move in a way to reach the creator's goal? Game theory focuses on how the player can maximize their utility under a game.

According to Justin Drake, Researcher at the Ethereum Foundation, The rule of thumb is that: "when you can use math, if not, use economics".

ZK Rollups in depth‚Äã

ZK Rollup is an L2 scaling solution in which all funds are held by a smart contract on the mainchain, while computation and storage are performed off-chain.

For every Rollup block, a state transition zero-knowledge proof (SNARK) is generated and verified by the mainchain contract.

This SNARK includes the proof of the validity of every single transaction in the Rollup block.

Additionally, the public data update for every block is published over the mainchain network in the cheap calldata.
